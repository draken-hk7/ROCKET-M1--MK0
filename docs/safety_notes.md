# Safety Notes

This repository is for education, documentation, and software automation only.
It must not be used to fabricate, pressure test, fuel, ignite, launch, or
hot-fire rocket hardware.

## Regulatory Orientation

Rocket propulsion work can be regulated by aviation authorities, explosives
laws, fire codes, environmental rules, transport rules, site permits, insurance
requirements, and institutional safety boards. Applicable authorities may
include FAA-style aviation regulators, BATFE-style explosives regulators, local
fire departments, range safety officers, and national or state agencies.

For India and international use, consult qualified legal and safety
professionals before any propulsion activity. This project does not determine
what propellants, oxidizers, pressure systems, or test operations are lawful or
safe in any jurisdiction.

## Safety Boundary

The following are intentionally absent:

- propellant preparation or handling instructions
- injector orifice sizing
- pressure vessel wall thickness
- ignition system design
- hot-fire sequence timing
- valve lead or lag values
- flight hardware tolerances
- pressure-rated drawings
- acceptance-test criteria

## Recommended Safe Uses

- classroom discussion of propulsion vocabulary
- software CI practice
- normalized plotting exercises
- CAD automation training with solid display models
- documentation and configuration-management practice

Any operational propulsion work belongs with certified facilities, qualified
engineers, approved procedures, and lawful supervision.
